Abstract

In this conversational Text-to-speech(CTTS) task, the heterogeneous graph has excellent emotional rendering effects for conversation speech synthesis. However, in the construction process of heterogeneous graphs, the impact of fine-grained contextual historical information on emotional communication during dialogue has not been considered, which has always been a challenge. To address this issue, this article proposes the Fine grained text feature enhanced emotion aware Conversational Text to Speech (FECTTS). Specifically, we utilized fine-grained word level text features and other modalities to jointly construct heterogeneous graphs. In order to obtain more accurate and delicate emotional features, we employed a simple and effective emotion predictor. The experimental results show that the method proposed in this article has excellent conversational speech synthesis quality, and contains rich emotional expressions in the synthesized current discourse that match the emotional expressions in the context.
